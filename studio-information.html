Studio information

Structure, goals, and tuition.

Harmonia Conservatory offers a balanced curriculum of piano repertoire, technique, music theory, ear training, and history. Every student receives an individualized lesson plan that grows with them.

Objectives

Balanced music education

1) Study of piano literature 2) Theory behind the notes 3) Healthy technique 4) Historical and storytelling context—all supported with a sense of humor.

Confidence

Poise & discipline

Students build self-esteem through guided practice habits, performance opportunities, and thoughtful preparation for every event.

Inspiration

Love every genre

Classical, jazz, soundtracks, pop, improvisation—the studio encourages exploring music that sparks curiosity.

Equipment checklist

  • • Tuned 88-key acoustic piano (minimum once per year) or high-quality digital piano with weighted keys
  • • Adequate lighting at the piano and a supportive, adjustable bench (add footstools or pads for younger students)
  • • Reliable metronome
  • • Notebook for practice steps and theory assignments

Lesson length

  • • Preparatory–Level 1: 30 minutes of private instruction + 15–20 minutes of computer lab
  • • Elementary (Level 2–3): 40 minutes private + 20 minute lab
  • • Intermediate (Level 4–5): 50 minutes private + 20 minute lab
  • • Level 6–Advanced: 60 minutes private + lab as needed

Tuition & materials

Tuition is based on a 38-week year and includes up to $60 of materials, three online studio classes, and two recitals. Forty-one lessons are scheduled annually to allow flexibility for occasional absences. Payments made after the 5th of the month incur a $20 late fee.

Payments may be made via Zelle, cash, or check (payable to Laurie Meinhold). Please email for the full tuition schedule.

Homeschool purchase orders are accepted monthly, and adult students may purchase lessons in groups of four.

Recitals & festivals

Studio recitals are held each fall and spring, featuring themes that highlight genres or composers. Students can participate in community service recitals at retirement homes along with MTAC festivals, duet celebrations, and competitions.

Performances may be virtual or in-person as needed. Young students play duets with Laurie to ease nerves, and every student receives individualized coaching.

MTAC Certificate of Merit™

The Certificate of Merit program includes video performances, online theory exams, and aural tests each February–March. Registration opens every September. Students earn certificates at each level and may be selected for State honors concerts.

Studio calendar

The studio follows the Capistrano Valley School District calendar for Spring Break, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving, Winter Break, and a short summer recess. Lessons continue on most other holidays to keep momentum going.
