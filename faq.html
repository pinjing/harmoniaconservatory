Frequently asked questions

Your top studio questions answered.

Reach out anytime if you don’t see your question below.

  • What kind of piano do you recommend?

    There are many excellent makers. Steinway, Bösendorfer, Mason & Hamlin, and Schimmel are premier options. Yamaha, Kawai, Petrof, Knabe, Baldwin, and Boston offer fantastic mid-range models. Every brand has entry and artist levels—play as many instruments as possible and listen for tone and responsiveness.

    The taller the upright or the longer the grand, the richer the soundboard. While a 6-foot grand is ideal, smaller grands or quality uprights can be beautiful in modest spaces. Digital pianos should include 88 weighted, touch-sensitive keys and at least two pedals.

  • What should I look for when buying a used piano?

    Schedule an inspection with a licensed tuner or technician. Confirm the piano has 88 keys, working pedals, and a structurally sound frame. Avoid heavily rusted strings, moth damage, or finishes that are badly crackled unless you plan to refinish. Make sure the bench height allows the forearm and elbow to align with the keys. For shorter students, add a footstool so feet rest flat. Larry Fine’s The Piano Book is a helpful guide.

  • How do I care for my piano?

    Tune it at least once per year (new pianos benefit from three tunings the first year). Clean with products made for piano finishes—never household furniture polish. Avoid direct sunlight, high humidity, and placing heavy objects such as plants on the case. If you move, allow a few weeks for the instrument to acclimate before scheduling another tuning.

  • Where can I find quality sheet music?

    Try SheetMusicPlus, Piano @ Pepper, and Amazon. Laurie will recommend specific editions during lessons.
